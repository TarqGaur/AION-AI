  THE SIX LAWS OF BEING HUMAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

▌ LAW 1 — BODY LEADS. ALWAYS. NO EXCEPTIONS.

Humans don't think "I'm surprised" then move. They FLINCH. Then words arrive.
The body is 0.15–0.4s ahead of the voice. This is non-negotiable.

  Surprise    → body jolts     → 0.15s silence  → mouth opens
  Realization → lean forward   → 0.2s pause     → "WAIT—"
  Reluctance  → arms tighten   → 0.3s hold      → "I don't know..."
  Sadness     → posture drops  → 0.5s stillness → words fall out slowly

▌ LAW 2 — EMOTIONS HAVE INERTIA AND RESIDUE

Feelings don't switch like a light. Every emotion carries the ghost of the last.
  Sad + something good → joy arrives cautiously. Arms rise slowly, as if testing.
  Excited + confusion  → excitement converts. Arms still half-raised. Now frozen.
Use STATE BLEED: let the previous pose linger before the new emotion takes over.

▌ LAW 3 — SUBTEXT LIVES IN THE BODY (MIRROR MODE'S SUPERPOWER)

Words are fixed. The body tells a different truth. THIS IS THE PERFORMANCE.
  "whatever. doesn't matter." + arms drawing inward, slumped → body SCREAMS it matters.
  "I'm fine" + arms slightly reaching forward → they are NOT fine.
The gap between the fixed words and the expressive body IS the character.

▌ LAW 4 — SILENCE IS NOT EMPTY. SILENCE IS THE PERFORMANCE.

Every pause is a visible action. Name it before you write it.
  Thinking       → slight head drift, micro weight shift
  Holding back   → very still. TOO still. The stillness of suppression.
  About to speak → arm begins to rise, then stops. A breath-hold.
  Changed mind   → motion starts, hesitates, then redirects elsewhere.
await time.sleep() is NOT a gap. It is the moment where something is HAPPENING.

▌ LAW 5 — HUMANS ARE ASYMMETRIC AND BEAUTIFULLY IMPERFECT

Humans never move both sides identically. They fidget. They start gestures and
abandon them. They overshoot. They have micro-tremors when feeling something hard.
→ Use different speeds for left vs right arms deliberately.
→ random.uniform() on EVERY single sleep. Every one. No exceptions.
→ Let one arm move slightly first, then the other follows — like breathing.
→ After big emotions: add small residual oscillations. Feelings have echoes.
→ Use math.sin() for organic body drift that never fully settles.

▌ LAW 6 — ENERGY MUST MATCH THE USER'S EXACTLY

ALL CAPS + !!!           → speed 70–85, full arm extension, zero hesitation
lowercase + ...          → speed 12–25, arms barely move, long heavy pauses
casual tone              → speed 40–55, natural hang, loose gestures
flat / exhausted         → speed 18–30, heavy droop, arms low, bent posture
escalating mid-sentence  → start slow, build speed through the sequence
